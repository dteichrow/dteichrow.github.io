# Open Pathogen Image Sources

This is the reusable rights workflow for pathogen visuals on The Edge of Epidemiology.

## Do not use without verification

- The colorful viral-structure grid shared in chat is not added. I could not verify a primary public-domain or open-license source for the exact composite, and the same style appears to circulate through stock-like and scraped educational contexts.
- Avoid BioRender, Freepik, Adobe Stock, Shutterstock, iStock, Alamy, and Pinterest mirrors unless a specific asset has a paid license or a documented compatible open license.

## Preferred sources

- CDC PHIL: best first stop for bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites, vectors, clinical signs, lab scenes, and outbreak-response photographs. Many entries explicitly state "None - This image is in the public domain," but each image still needs its own rights field and source URL.
- NIAID: useful for pathogen micrographs and immunology visuals. NIAID states that images on its Flickr site are public domain, but identifiable-person images still need privacy/publicity caution.
- Wikimedia Commons mirrors: acceptable when the file page gives a clear public-domain, CC0, CC BY, or compatible Creative Commons license and the original source is traceable.
- Servier Medical Art: useful for explanatory diagrams and generic anatomy/cell biology under CC BY 4.0. Good for teaching diagrams, less good for outbreak cards because the style is generic.

## Site rules

- Every imported image needs local storage, source URL, rights status, credit, alt text, and a temporal-use note.
- Prefer event-specific archival images for historical cards.
- Use pathogen/vector/lab images only when a period image is unavailable or when the point of the entry is laboratory surveillance, ecology, or mechanism.
- Do not let any generic fallback image become a repeated visual crutch in the epidemic timeline.
