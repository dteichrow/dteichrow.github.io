Card 01

Multiple choice

Which evidence source does the essay say can identify pathogens directly in Viking Age remains?

  1. Acemetery evidence
  2. Bfine-grained osteological evidence
  3. CDNA or bone evidence
  4. DAncient DNA

Answer 01

Ancient DNA

Ancient DNA has changed this a bit because it can sometimes identify pathogens directly with the most striking example coming from smallpox-related variola virus strains found in Viking Age samples.

Card 02

Multiple choice

Which option best completes this essay point? "...narrower occupational group partaking in sea-raiding, trading, and warfare, this piece isn’t totally about them since most _____ is more about the Viking Age Norse and Scandinavian groups."

  1. AAncient DNA
  2. Bfine-grained osteological evidence
  3. CDNA or bone evidence
  4. Dcemetery evidence

Answer 02

cemetery evidence

If by Viking we mean the narrower occupational group partaking in sea-raiding, trading, and warfare, this piece isn’t totally about them since most cemetery evidence is more about the Viking Age Norse and Scandinavian groups.

Card 03

Fill in the blank

Fill in the blank: "The burial evidence that has been quantified is also a bit too biased to give us any sort of clean, authoritative Viking life _____."

Answer 03

table or actuarial analysis

The burial evidence that has been quantified is also a bit too biased to give us any sort of clean, authoritative Viking life table or actuarial analysis.

Card 04

Multiple choice

Which dental condition does the essay identify as probably widespread?

  1. Aperiapical disease
  2. Bkidney or liver disease
  3. Chigh blood pressure
  4. Dbipolar disorder

Answer 04

periapical disease

The one condition we can say was probably widespread was infection around the root of the tooth, as 12 of 15 individuals examined had some form of periapical disease.

Card 05

Short answer

What does the essay say the available evidence is good enough to reconstruct?

Answer 05

That said, there is enough evidence out there for us to have a rough look at what life and death may have looked like for the average Viking Age Norse and the sea-faring warriors we call the Vikings.

Card 06

Multiple choice

Which outbreak metric does the essay say remains unknown?

  1. Alife table style analysis
  2. Battack rate
  3. Ccase fatality rate
  4. Dchildhood mortality

Answer 06

case fatality rate

We still don’t know anything regarding the attack rate, case fatality rate, what the epidemic curve could have looked like, or how it behaved compared to later smallpox strains.

Card 07

Multiple choice

  1. AAncient DNA
  2. Bcemetery evidence
  3. CDNA or bone evidence

Answer 07

Infant bones don’t preserve super well, and cremation can strip away so much of the fine-grained osteological evidence that we’d want and need to create one of those.

Card 08

Fill in the blank

Fill in the blank: "Bones, teeth, isotopes, and _____ give us enough to have a general idea about how they lived, got sick, got hurt, and died."

Answer 08

ancient DNA

Bones, teeth, isotopes, and ancient DNA give us enough to have a general idea about how they lived, got sick, got hurt, and died.

Card 09

Short answer

Why can skeletal evidence miss many infectious causes of death?

Answer 09

People can die of a variety of diseases like diarrheal disease, pneumonia, sepsis, puerperal infection, influenza, respiratory infection, or even a battle wound infection and we see no trace at all in the bones.

Answer 10

childhood mortality

Infants and young children also tend to preserve less well and could have been buried under different practices due to the high rates of childhood mortality at the time.
